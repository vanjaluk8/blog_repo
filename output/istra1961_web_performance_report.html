The audit combined automated lab measurements (Google Lighthouse) with real-user field data (Google PageSpeed Insights / Chrome UX Report) to provide a complete picture of how the club's digital presence performs in practice.

Detailed Findings

Below is a narrative walk-through of each major issue identified, explaining what it means in plain terms and why it matters for the club.

🐢

1. The page loads too slowly — and it costs the club fans

When a visitor lands on nkistra.com, they wait over 3.4 seconds before anything appears on screen, and over 8.1 seconds before the main content (hero image or article) is fully visible. On a mobile device, lab simulations record a staggering 52.7 seconds to complete loading — a figure that reflects how punishing the site's current build is for slower mobile connections.

Research by Google consistently shows that 53% of mobile visitors abandon a site that takes longer than 3 seconds to load. For a club competing in Croatia's top flight, losing more than half of mobile visitors before they see a single piece of content represents a measurable and avoidable loss of audience engagement.

📦

2. The page is carrying unnecessary weight — 2.1 MB of unused code

Every time a visitor loads nkistra.com, their browser downloads 12.1 MB of data. Of that, 2.14 MB is CSS code that is never used on the page, and a further 898 KB of JavaScript is loaded but not executed. This is the digital equivalent of sending a full catalogue when the customer only needed one page.

These unused assets directly increase load times for everyone, but disproportionately affect fans browsing on mobile data. Removing or deferring this dead code is one of the highest-return optimisations available and requires no redesign of the site.

🔒

3. Critical resources are blocking the page from rendering

The audit identified resources — primarily scripts and stylesheets — that force the browser to pause and wait before it can draw anything on screen. These render-blocking resources are responsible for an estimated 2,817 ms (2.8 seconds) of delay on every page load.

This means that nearly three seconds of every visitor's wait time could theoretically be eliminated simply by reordering how the page loads its dependencies — without changing a single visible element of the design.

📐

4. Content jumps around as the page loads — damaging user trust

The Cumulative Layout Shift score of 0.216 means that elements on the page — text, images, buttons — visibly move and reflow as the page completes loading. This is the experience where a user tries to tap a link and the page shifts at the last moment, causing them to tap the wrong thing.

Beyond user frustration, CLS is a confirmed ranking factor in Google's search algorithm. A score of 0.216 places the site in Google's "Poor" category (good is below 0.1), which can suppress the site's visibility in search results for terms like "NK Istra 1961", "Pula football", or "HNL tickets".

🖥️

5. The server responds slowly and is hosted outside Croatia

The website's server takes 870 ms (nearly a full second) before it begins sending any data to a visitor's browser. The real-user median (p75) wait is even higher, at 1,491 ms on mobile and 1,441 ms on desktop.

Investigation reveals the server is hosted with Contabo GmbH, a budget hosting provider, on infrastructure physically located in Lauterbourg, France. While this may reduce hosting costs, it introduces additional network latency for every visitor in Croatia, and budget hosting typically means shared resources with no performance guarantees — a poor fit for a club whose website traffic spikes sharply on matchdays.

Additionally, the site is running on HTTP/1.1 — an older protocol. Upgrading to HTTP/2 or HTTP/3 would allow the browser to load multiple files simultaneously, significantly reducing overall load time at no content cost.

🏗️

6. The page's underlying structure is extremely complex

The page contains 1,665 DOM elements — the individual building blocks of the webpage. Google considers anything above 800 elements a warning, and above 1,400 a critical issue. At 1,665, nkistra.com exceeds even the critical threshold.

An over-complex page structure forces the browser to work harder on every scroll and interaction, slowing down the entire experience. It is typically a symptom of a page template that has accumulated functionality over time without periodic cleanup — something that a structured frontend review could address.

✅

7. Interactivity is a genuine strength

One bright spot: once the page has loaded, it responds quickly to user interactions. The Interaction to Next Paint (INP) score of 98 ms on mobile and 59 ms on desktop places the site in Google's "Fast" category for interactivity — a real positive that indicates the underlying JavaScript is reasonably well-structured once it has finished loading.

This tells us the site's core problems are in delivery (how assets are loaded), not in the application logic itself — meaning targeted optimisations could yield significant improvements without a full rebuild.

Recommendations

The following actions are prioritised by expected impact. Items marked P1 will deliver the largest improvement with reasonable effort. Many of these can be implemented by a competent web developer without rebuilding the site from scratch.

P1 — Critical

Upgrade hosting to a modern provider with Croatian/regional presence

Move from Contabo (budget, France) to a managed hosting provider — such as Cloudflare Pages, Vercel, or a Croatian/regional cloud — with HTTP/2 support, built-in CDN edge caching, and a server response time SLA. This single change could cut TTFB from ~870 ms to under 100 ms.

Expected improvement: Server response time −80%
P1 — Critical

Remove or defer unused CSS and JavaScript

Audit and eliminate the 2.14 MB of unused CSS and 898 KB of unused JavaScript. This likely requires a one-time review of the site's theme and plugins. Tools like PurgeCSS and code-splitting can automate much of this. Alone, this change could halve the page's total data weight.

Expected improvement: Page weight −25–40%, load time −2–4 s
P1 — Critical

Eliminate render-blocking resources

Move non-critical JavaScript to load asynchronously (async/defer) and inline critical CSS. This will allow the browser to begin painting the screen immediately rather than waiting 2.8 seconds for scripts to execute. A developer can implement this in a day.

Expected improvement: FCP −2 s, LCP −1–2 s
P1 — Critical

Fix layout shift — reserve space for images and ads

Ensure every image, video embed, and advertisement has explicit width/height attributes or CSS aspect-ratio rules. This prevents content from shifting as media loads. Fixing the CLS from 0.216 to below 0.1 will directly improve Google Search ranking.

Expected improvement: CLS <0.1, SEO ranking lift
P2 — High

Enable HTTP/2 and image compression

Upgrading from HTTP/1.1 to HTTP/2 allows the browser to download all 107 page resources in parallel rather than sequentially. Combine with modern image formats (WebP/AVIF) and lazy loading for below-the-fold images to further reduce load time.

Expected improvement: Load time −1–2 s on all connections
P2 — High

Reduce DOM complexity

Work with the development team to review the page template and reduce the DOM element count from 1,665 toward Google's recommended ceiling of 800. This typically involves consolidating redundant wrapper elements and removing hidden or legacy content blocks from the markup.

Expected improvement: Scroll/interaction performance, mobile experience
P3 — Recommended

Implement performance monitoring

Set up ongoing automated performance tracking (e.g. via Google Search Console's Core Web Vitals report, or a tool such as SpeedCurve). This ensures regressions are caught before they impact fans — especially important around high-traffic periods like matchdays and transfer windows.

Expected improvement: Ongoing quality assurance
P3 — Recommended

Conduct a full mobile UX audit

Given that mobile real-user LCP is rated "Slow" by Google, commission a dedicated mobile usability review. Many performance fixes will also unblock UX improvements — particularly around the navigation, news feed, and ticketing journey for fans on smartphones.

Expected improvement: Fan engagement, bounce rate reduction
Conclusion

NK Istra 1961's website currently ranks 8th out of 10 HNL SuperSport clubs on core web performance metrics. The gap between Istra's current score (45) and the league leaders Dinamo and Rijeka (82) is substantial but entirely closeable — and the technical barriers are well understood.

The root causes are not design failures — the site is visually complete and its interactivity is genuinely strong. The problems lie in how assets are delivered: outdated hosting infrastructure, accumulated unused code, and a lack of modern delivery practices that competing clubs have already addressed.

Addressing the P1 recommendations in this report — hosting migration, dead-code removal, and render-blocking fixes — could realistically move the performance score from 45 to 70+, bring the site inside Google's "Good" thresholds for real users, and improve search visibility for all club-related queries. These changes are achievable within a 4–6 week engagement with a single experienced web developer.

In a competitive landscape where clubs like Dinamo, Rijeka, and Hajduk already operate high-performing digital properties, a fast and reliable website is no longer a technical luxury — it is part of the club's public face. Every second of unnecessary loading time is a fan who didn't see the latest match report, a sponsor who clicked away, or a potential supporter who formed their first impression of the club from a blank screen.
